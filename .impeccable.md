## Design Context

### Users
Primary users are runners and cyclists who want one place to see, clean up, and control training data imported from different providers. The core jobs are reconciling provider data, resolving inconsistencies, and managing gear usage and wear.

### Brand Personality
Technical, utilitarian, precise.

The interface should feel calm and reliable while users work through messy data. It should support focused operational work rather than entertainment, social engagement, or motivation loops.

### Aesthetic Direction
The product should feel like a calm data notebook with some athletic energy. Light mode is the primary experience.

Relevant influences:
- Strava: recognizable sport cues
- COROS: technical training tone
- Google Health: calm, clean, low-friction layout

Anti-direction:
- not playful
- not social-fitness
- avoid purple and pink accents

### Design Principles
1. Make operational tasks feel clear and low-friction; reduce ambiguity before adding personality.
2. Prefer calm, clean layouts with enough space to think, rather than dense dashboard clutter.
3. Keep the tone technical and precise, but never harsh or overly corporate.
4. Use athletic cues sparingly and purposefully to support recognition, not decoration.
5. Default to light, readable surfaces and avoid trend-driven palettes or glossy social-fitness styling.
